Essential Money Tips

🏦 Open the Right Bank Account First

Before anything else, open a free checking account with no monthly fees and no minimum balance. Best options for students: Chime, Ally, or a local credit union. Also open a separate savings account — no debit card attached.

💳 The Credit Card Rules (All 3 Are Non-Negotiable)

Use it only for things you'd buy anyway. Pay the full balance every single month — not the minimum. Keep the limit at $500 to protect yourself from yourself. The best starter cards: Discover it Student or Capital One Quicksilver Student.

⚠️ The Refund Is Not Free Money

If your refund comes from student loans, that money is borrowed. Every dollar you spend on non-essentials is a dollar you'll repay with 5–8% interest. Transfer it immediately to a separate account and allocate it before you touch a cent.

📚 Never Buy Textbooks Full Price

Before buying any textbook, check: your library, Chegg, VitalSource, PDF groups, or older editions. Textbooks can cost $300–$600 a semester at full price. You can often get them for under $50 with the right approach.

📅 The 5-Minute Sunday Check-In

Every Sunday, open your bank app. Check your balance. Log any expenses you haven't tracked. Know where you stand for the week ahead. Five minutes. Every week. This single habit prevents almost every financial crisis.

✈️ Book Holiday Travel Early

Thanksgiving and winter break flights booked last-minute can cost $300–$600+. Book as soon as your school calendar is published — usually by September. Early booking can cut costs by 50% or more.

🎓 Use Every Student Discount

Your .edu email is worth hundreds of dollars a year. Amazon Prime Student (half price), Spotify Student, Apple Music Student, Microsoft Office (often free through your school), Adobe Creative Cloud discounts, and hundreds of local business deals. Always ask.

🚫 The Overdraft Fee Trap

Banks charge $25–$35 per overdraft — and they can hit you multiple times in a single day. Set up low balance alerts at $100. Better: choose a bank with no overdraft fees (like Chime) entirely. One overdraft habit can cost $100+/month.
